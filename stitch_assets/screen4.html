Primary Case Study

Large-Scale Freeze-Thaw Plate Loading Tests

Experimental Analysis of Thermal and Mechanical Stresses in Subgrade Infrastructure.

target

Research Objectives

The primary objective of this study is to investigate the complex freeze-thaw effects on soil stability, specifically focusing on the cyclical transitions between frozen and unfrozen states in controlled laboratory conditions.

  • check
    Quantification of stress distribution during extreme thermal cycling across multi-layered soil structures.
  • check
    Observation of pore-water pressure variations during active thaw phases to identify potential stability loss.
  • check
    Validation of numerical models using large-scale physical test data from plate loading sequences.
psychology

Research Motivation

Climate change and industrial development in sub-arctic regions have increased the urgency for resilient infrastructure. Understanding frost heave and thaw weakening is critical for the long-term integrity of highways, railways, and pipelines.

Frost Heave

Vertical displacement caused by ice lens formation during the freezing front penetration.

Thaw Weakening

Critical reduction in soil bearing capacity due to rapid saturation during seasonal warming.
