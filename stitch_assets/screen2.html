Experimental Methodology

A structured two-phase protocol for assessing material durability under extreme environmental and mechanical stress. The following sequence details thermal cycling and subsequent structural load testing within the reaction frame assembly.

01

Freeze-thaw Phase

Thermal Integrity Protocol

ac_unit

Samples are subjected to controlled thermal cycling to simulate extreme environmental degradation. This phase focuses on the application of thermal insulation barriers and precise temperature modulation via the centralized chiller system.

Specific Procedural Steps

  • 1 Apply insulation blankets to sample chamber (R-value > 6.0).
  • 2 Calibrate T1-T4 thermal sensors at 450mm depth intervals.
  • 3 Execute 4-hour soak at -80°C followed by 2°C/min thawing.

Technical Specifications

Minimum Temp. -80.0 °C
Cycle Duration 04:45:00
Relative Humidity 45% ± 2%
02

Plate Loading Test Phase

Structural Capacity Test

precision_manufacturing

Mechanical load application using the dynamic actuator and reaction frame. This phase quantifies the residual structural integrity of the material after the freeze-thaw sequence has concluded.

Specific Procedural Steps

  • 1 Initialize dynamic actuator and zero-point alignment.
  • 2 Position aluminum loading plates (96 well-point grid).
  • 3 Incremental force application with force-feedback loop.

Technical Specifications

Max Actuator Force 12.5 kN
Loading Tolerance ± 0.05 mm
